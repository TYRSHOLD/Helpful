## Helpful – User Guide

This guide is written for **students using the Helpful app**, not for developers. It explains what each part of the app does and how to get the most out of it.

---

## Getting Started

- **Sign up** with your email and a password.
- After signing up, you’ll go through a short **onboarding** flow where you enter:
  - Your name
  - Birthday (optional)
  - School and major
- Once you finish onboarding, you’ll land on the **Home** screen.

You stay signed in between launches. You can sign out any time from **Home → profile icon**.

---

## Home (Dashboard)

The **Home** tab gives you a quick snapshot of your money:

- A **greeting** with your name and daily streak.
- **Summary cards**:
  - **Budget** – total budget you’ve set for your current months.
  - **Spent** – how much you’ve spent.
  - **Saved / Goals / Opportunities** – shortcuts into those tabs.
- **Quick Actions**:
  - Add a transaction
  - Jump to Budgets, Documents, Tools, or Discover
- **Recent Transactions**:
  - Shows your latest purchases.
  - Swipe left to delete.
  - Tap to see full details (amount, date, note, receipt).

---

## Bottom Tabs Overview

From left to right, the main tabs are:

- **Home** – dashboard, quick actions, and recent activity.
- **Budgets** – set and track monthly spending limits.
- **Spending** – higher‑level charts and a breakdown of where your money goes.
- **Goals** – savings goals (e.g. spring break, laptop, emergency fund).
- **Docs** – upload and store important documents like financial aid letters.
- **Tools** – GPA calculator and semester cost planner.
- **Discover** – swipe to find scholarships and internships.
- **Transactions** – full list of your income and spending with search and export.

The rest of this guide explains the important ones in more detail.

---

## Budgets

**What it’s for:** setting a monthly spending limit and seeing how close you are.

On the **Budgets** tab you can:

- **Add a budget** – choose a month and total amount.
- See:
  - Total amount budgeted
  - How much you’ve **spent**
  - How much is **remaining**
- **Progress bars** show how close you are to your limit.
- Swipe left on a budget to **delete** it.

Helpful automatically updates your current month’s budget when you **add or delete expense transactions**.

---

## Spending

**What it’s for:** understanding your income, spending, and trends over time.

### Time Range & Month Strip

At the top you’ll see:

- A **time range** selector: **Week, Month, Quarter, Year**.
- A horizontal strip of **months**, each with two mini bars:
  - Blue = **Income**
  - Grey = **Total Spend**
- Tap a month to select it. The summary and breakdown will update for that period.

### Summary Cards

Below the strip you’ll see three rows:

- **Income** – how much money came in during the selected period.
- **Total Spent** – how much you spent (expenses only).
  - Tap this row to jump straight to the **Transactions** tab so you can see every transaction behind that total.
- **Net Income** – Income − Total Spent.
  - Positive net income means you saved money.
  - Negative net income means you spent more than you brought in.
  - Tap the **Net Income** row (or its info icon) to see:
    - A plain‑language explanation of net income.
    - A worked example using your numbers for the current period.

### Breakdown Ring

The **Breakdown** section shows a thin, clean donut chart:

- Center text shows:
  - “Total spend in [Month]”
  - The total amount you spent in that month.
- Each colored slice represents a **spending category** (Food, Transport, Shopping, etc.).
- Below the chart is a list that shows, for each category:
  - Percentage of total spend
  - Dollar amount spent

You can toggle **Include bills** on or off:

- When off, the **Bills** category is excluded from the breakdown so you can focus on everyday spending.

## Transactions

**What it’s for:** the detailed history behind all your money moves.

The **Transactions** tab shows a full list of both **income** and **expenses**.

- **Search bar** at the top:
  - Search by category name, note, or amount.
- **Filters**:
  - Use the filter icon to show all categories or a specific one (e.g. Food only).
- **Grouped by month**:
  - Each section header shows the month and a net total for that month.
- **Rows**:
  - Left: category icon (e.g. fork & knife for Food).
  - Middle: title (usually the note, otherwise the category) and date.
  - Right: amount:
    - Green and prefixed with **+** for income.
    - Coral and prefixed with **−** for expenses.
    - Labelled “Income” or “Expense” underneath.
- **Interactions**:
  - Tap a row to see full transaction details, including the receipt image if you captured one.
  - Swipe left to delete; if it’s an expense, your budget is adjusted automatically.
  - Tap the **+** button in the bottom‑right corner to add a new transaction.

### Adding a Transaction

When you tap **Add**:

- Enter:
  - Amount
  - Category (Food, Transport, etc.)
  - Type: **Expense** or **Income**
  - Optional note (e.g. “North Italia dinner with friends”)
  - Optional receipt photo from your library or camera
- You can mark a transaction as **recurring** (Weekly, Biweekly, Monthly).
- Save it:
  - If it’s an **expense**, Helpful will also add that amount to the current month’s budget spending.

## Goals

**What it’s for:** saving toward things that matter to you.

On the **Goals** tab you can:

- Create a new goal by providing:
  - Title (e.g. “Spring Break Trip”)
  - Emoji icon
  - Target amount
  - Deadline date
- Each goal card shows:
  - Current amount saved vs. target
  - A progress bar and remaining amount
- You can:
  - Add money toward a goal.
  - Swipe left to delete a goal once it’s no longer needed.

---

## Documents

**What it’s for:** keeping important money‑related documents in one place.

On the **Docs** tab you can:

- Upload PDFs and images, such as:
  - Financial aid letters
  - Tuition bills
  - Pay stubs or offer letters
- Each row shows:
  - Title
  - File name
  - Upload date
- Tap a document to open it.
- Swipe left to delete.

Files are stored securely in Firebase Storage; only you (signed in with your account) can see your documents.

---

## Tools (GPA & Cost Calculators)

The **Tools** tab contains two helpers:

- **GPA Calculator**:
  - Add courses with name, credits, and letter grade.
  - The app calculates your semester GPA as you go.
  - Swipe left on a course to remove it.
- **Cost Calculator**:
  - Add line items for tuition, housing, food, transport, books, and anything else you need.
  - See a running total cost for the semester.
  - Swipe left to remove items and tap the add button to add more.

These tools don’t currently affect your budgets – they’re for planning and “what‑if” scenarios.

---

## Discover (Scholarships & Internships)

**What it’s for:** quickly browsing opportunities that can earn or save you money.

The **Discover** tab has two main parts:

- **Discover**:
  - Tinder‑style swipe cards that show scholarships and internships.
  - Swipe **right** to save, **left** to dismiss.
  - Use the buttons at the bottom to:
    - Dismiss (X)
    - Undo the last swipe
    - Save (heart)
  - Filter between **All**, **Scholarships**, and **Internships**.
- **Saved**:
  - A list of all opportunities you’ve saved.
  - Tap a row to open the application link in Safari.
  - Swipe left to unsave if you’re no longer interested.

Your saved and dismissed opportunities are synced to the cloud so you don’t lose them if you reinstall.

---

## Profile & Settings

You can reach your profile from the **Home** screen by tapping the **person** icon in the top‑right.

In your profile you can:

- Edit your **name**, **school**, and **major**.
- Toggle:
  - **Notifications**
  - **Dark mode** (or follow system appearance)
- Sign out of your account.

Changes are saved to your profile in the backend so they follow you between devices.

---

## Tips & Best Practices

- **Log transactions quickly** – use the “Add Transaction” quick action on Home so budgets and spending stay accurate.
- **Create a budget every month** – if you like your previous month’s budget, Helpful can roll it over automatically.
- **Check Spending weekly** – the Spending tab is designed to be your “money check‑in”:
  - See if your net income is positive.
  - See which categories are eating most of your budget.
- **Use Goals for motivation** – saving toward something specific is more motivating than saving “just because”.
- **Keep key docs in Docs** – financial aid letters, bills, and pay stubs are easy to lose in email; Docs keeps them in one place.
